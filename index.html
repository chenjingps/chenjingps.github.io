Jing Chen

Professor
School of Psychology, Shanghai University of Sport

I am a professor of psychology. My research focuses on visual perception, color vision, eye movements, technology use, and mental health. I use questionnaires, behavioral experiments, eye-tracking, electrophysiological methods (including ERP and SSVEP), and computational modeling to study how people perceive dynamic environments and how they engage with digital technologies.

Research

Visual Perception

I study visual perception, color vision, and eye movements using EEG and eye-tracking to investigate the neural and cognitive mechanisms of visual processing in dynamic environments.

Teaching

My teaching spans undergraduate and graduate courses in psychology, cognitive neuroscience, and research methods. I adopt a student-centered approach that emphasizes engagement and hands-on learning. Recent teaching work also addresses the challenges and opportunities created by generative AI in education.

Courses

Graduate: Advanced Cognitive Neuroscience
